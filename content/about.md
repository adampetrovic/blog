# About

I'm Adam Petrovic, a senior engineer at Atlassian based in Sydney, Australia.

I work on software systems professionally, and I run too many of them at home on purpose. My interests sit around the practical edge of engineering: Kubernetes, GitOps, automation, developer tooling, observability, reliability, and the small operational details that decide whether something is pleasant to run after the novelty wears off.

My homelab is where a lot of that thinking gets tested. It is a bare-metal Kubernetes environment managed with Talos Linux, Flux CD, Helm, Kustomize, SOPS, Rook-Ceph, VolSync, Envoy Gateway, and a growing collection of self-hosted services. It is equal parts infrastructure, workshop, and failure generator.

I keep most of that work in Git. Some of the public pieces are on [GitHub](https://github.com/adampetrovic), including:

- [`home-ops`](https://github.com/adampetrovic/home-ops), my Kubernetes and GitOps homelab repository
- [`hass-config`](https://github.com/adampetrovic/hass-config), my Home Assistant configuration
- [`dotfiles`](https://github.com/adampetrovic/dotfiles), my personal environment managed with chezmoi
- smaller experiments, automations, and tools that escaped the private folder

This blog is a place for the write-ups that do not fit neatly into a commit message: what I changed, why I changed it, what broke, what I misunderstood, and what I would do differently next time.

Expect notes about:

- Kubernetes and GitOps
- Homelab operations
- Self-hosting
- Home automation
- Developer tooling
- Observability and reliability
- Practical engineering trade-offs

The style here is intentionally plain: minimal design, no tracking, no heavy frontend, and no performance theatre. Just field notes from building and running systems.

You can also find me on [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/adampetrovic/).
